Understanding the Golden Hour

The critical time period following a traumatic injury during which medical treatment has the highest likelihood of preventing death.

Phone Number Ambulance

Every minute counts in an emergency

Critical Conditions & Golden Hour Timeline

Stroke

Treatment within 3-4.5 hours is crucial to minimize brain damage and improve recovery outcomes.

3-4.5 hours

Heart Attack

Receiving treatment within 90 minutes significantly increases survival rates and reduces long-term damage.

90 min

Trauma

The original "golden hour" - trauma patients who receive care within 60 minutes have the best outcomes.

60 min

Severe Bleeding

Control of bleeding within the first 10-15 minutes is critical to prevent irreversible shock.

10-15 min

Why Time Matters

The concept of the "golden hour" was first described by R Adams Cowley, who recognized that trauma patients had the highest chance of survival if they received definitive care within 60 minutes of injury.

Today, we understand that different medical emergencies have different critical time windows, but the principle remains: rapid intervention saves lives.

Recognize the Signs

Stroke (FAST)

Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulties, Time to call emergency

Heart Attack

Chest pain/pressure, Pain in arms/back/jaw, Shortness of breath, Cold sweat, Nausea

Severe Trauma

Uncontrolled bleeding, Confusion, Rapid breathing, Pale/cool skin
